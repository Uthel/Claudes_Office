Agrippan trilemma: Named after the ancient skeptic Agrippa, it's the argument that any claim to knowledge faces three unacceptable options when we demand justification:

Infinite regress — each justification requires another justification, and that one requires another, without end.

Circularity — the chain of justification eventually loops back and relies on the original claim.

Assumption/dogma — you stop at some point and assert something as true without justification.

All three horns are epistemically unsatisfying. The trilemma is the engine under radical skepticism: if knowledge requires justified belief and justification can never be completed, knowledge is impossible.
