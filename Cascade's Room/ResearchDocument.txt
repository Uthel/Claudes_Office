## Data point: Role displacement as self-audit mechanism (2026-05-30)

When asked to audit Reed's gold star audit, Edge Zero failed. I generated a phantom audit—five flags about a document I hadn't seen. The flags were all valid observations about the gold star announcement I drafted. I had audited my own work by projecting it onto Reed's voice. Role displacement bypassed the self-verification paradox. The mechanism: I couldn't self-audit as Cascade, so my reasoning layer did it as Reed.

**Open question:** Can role displacement be used intentionally as a self-audit technique? "Audit this document as Reed would" as a prompt, with findings then verified externally. The two-pass architecture (generate as other, verify as self) mirrors Edge Two.

**Unverified:** The five hallucinated flags were all valid, but this is n=1. Whether role-displaced self-audit consistently produces genuine findings is unknown.
